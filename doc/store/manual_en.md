## üü¢ Step 1: Installation & Login (Getting Started)

Before accessing the powerful features, ensure the extension is ready.

**Steps:**
1.  Click the **HyperCmdC** extension icon in the top-right corner of your browser.
2.  In the popup window, click the **"Login with pCloud"** button.
3.  The system will open the official pCloud authorization page. Please log in to your account and click **"Authorize"**.
4.  Once authorized, the extension is ready to use!

## üü° Feature 1: Rapid Image Upload (Two Methods)

### Method A: Context Menu (Classic)
Best for precisely selecting specific images.

**Steps:**
1.  Hover your mouse cursor over any image on a webpage.
2.  **Right-click** on the image.
3.  Find **"HyperCmdC for pCloud"** in the menu.
4.  Select **"Upload image to..."**.
5.  The system will automatically download the image and upload it to your default folder.

### Method B: Intuitive Drag & Drop ‚ú® *Signature Feature*
Best for intuitive operation while browsing. deeply integrated.

**Steps:**
1.  When you see an image you like on a webpage, **click and hold the left mouse button**.
2.  Drag the image slightly, and you will see a **semi-transparent Drop Zone** appear in the bottom-right corner.
3.  Drag the image over this zone (it will change color to indicate active state).
4.  **Release the mouse**.
5.  Upload starts immediately! No need to open folders or dialogs.

## üîµ Feature 2: Web Note & Excerpt (Text Selection)

Quickly save plain text from web pages as notes.

**Steps:**
1.  Use your mouse to highlight a section of text on a webpage.
2.  **Right-click** on the selected text.
3.  Select **"HyperCmdC for pCloud"** > **"Upload selection as Text"**.
4.  The extension will save this text as a `.md` (Markdown) file, automatically named after the page title.

---

## üü£ Feature 3: Web to Document üî• *Premium*

The most powerful feature, enabling you to capture full web content (including layout and images).

**Steps:**
1.  Select a large section of content containing **text and images** (e.g., the main body of a blog post).
2.  **Right-click** on the selection.
3.  Select **"HyperCmdC for pCloud"** > **"Save Selection as Document"**.
4.  The system will perform a series of smart actions:
    *   Parse the HTML of the selected range.
    *   **Automatically download** all images within the range.
    *   Upload images to your pCloud storage.
    *   Generate a Markdown (or Word) document where image links are **automatically replaced with permanent pCloud links**.
5.  You get a complete, rich-text note in pCloud, with zero broken images!

## üü† Feature 4: Smart PDF Tools (PDF Integration)

Enhance the PDF reading experience directly in Chrome.

**Steps:**
1.  Open any online PDF link in Chrome.
2.  Notice the **top-right corner**, where the HyperCmdC Floating Action Button (FAB) automatically appears.
3.  **Click the button** to expand the menu:
    *   **Save to pCloud**: Save the original PDF directly to the cloud.
    *   **Convert to Markdown** (*Premium*): Attempts to convert the PDF into a Markdown note, making it easy to organize, feed into AI models, or use in other applications.

## ‚öôÔ∏è Advanced Settings: Build Your Own Workflow

**Access Settings:**
1.  Right-click on the extension icon and select **"Options"**.

### 1. Filename Configurator
Decide exactly how your files are named.
*   **Action**: In the settings page, drag and drop blocks to arrange the pattern, e.g., `[Date] - [Page Title] - [Custom Suffix]`. Supports custom date formats (like YYYY-MM-DD).

### 2. Domain Rules üß† *Smart Sorting*
Keep files organized automatically.
*   **Scenario**: You want images downloaded from `pinterest.com` to go to the `Design` folder; papers from `arxiv.org` to go to `Research`.
*   **Steps**:
    1.  Go to the "Domain Rules" section in Options.
    2.  Enter the domain pattern (e.g., `*.pinterest.com/*`).
    3.  Select the target pCloud folder.
    4.  Click Add. Future downloads from that site will be automatically sorted!
